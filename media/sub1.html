Our History

Founded in 2002, SpaceX is pioneering reusable rockets and redefining access to space.
From Falcon 1 to Starship, every breakthrough brings humanity closer to becoming a multiplanetary species.

  • Falcon1 First Success
    In 2008, SpaceX became the first private company to successfully launch a liquid-fueled rocket, Falcon 1. This milestone broke the long-standing belief that only government agencies could reach space. Though small in scale, Falcon 1’s success marked the beginning of SpaceX’s ambitious vision for the future.
  • Falcon 9 Reusability
    In 2015, the Falcon 9’s first stage safely returned to Earth, achieving the world’s first orbital-class rocket reusability. This breakthrough dramatically reduced launch costs and paved the way for the era of commercial spaceflight. Reusability has since become the cornerstone of SpaceX’s technological leadership.
  • Crew Dragon Launch
    In 2020, Crew Dragon launched astronauts to the International Space Station (ISS), becoming the first crewed spacecraft developed by a private company. This achievement, in collaboration with NASA, restored America’s human spaceflight capability. It established SpaceX as more than a launch provider—an essential partner in humanity’s journey to space.
  • Present Starship Development
    SpaceX is now accelerating the development of Starship. As the most powerful rocket ever built, Starship is designed to enable Mars colonization and revolutionize space logistics. While still in the testing phase, its success would open a new chapter in humanity’s exploration of the universe.
